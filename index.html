Images above are examples of the `gravitational edge instability' - a gravitational instability in astrophysical disks that is associated with edges or gaps. The GEI is well estabilished in the galatic context for stellar disks, but it's only recently made its way into protoplanetary disks. Gaps opened by giant planets in massive disks may be unstable to the GEI, even if the disk is stable according to the Toomre criterion. The GEI significantly affects planet migration, and may be an additional issue of concern for theoretical models that explain planet formation on wide orbits by disk fragmentation.
